The History of the Basilica

A Preservation of the grandeur and heritage of Catholic Basilicas through images!

Fact: In Ancient Roman architecture, a basilica (Greek basilike) was a large public building with multiple functions that was typically built alongside the towns forum. The basilica was in the Latin West equivalent to a stoa in the Greek East. The building gave its name to the basilica architectural form.

~By Nicolette Nyaboke

Curated Exhibition

A showcase displaying captured moments of historical grandeur:

  1. Basílica de la Sagrada Família

    The Sagrada Família is a massive basilica in Barcelona, Spain. It began as a traditional neo-Gothic church in 1882. When visionary architect Antoni Gaudí took over in 1883, he transformed it into a masterpiece of nature, light, and geometry. It is famous for taking over 140 years to build.
    Wouldn't you like to visit?

  2. Basilica of Our Lady of Licheń

    The Basilica of Our Lady of Licheń is the largest church in Poland and the eleventh largest in the world. Located in the small rural village of Licheń Stary near Konin, this monumental Roman Catholic basilica serves as a premier European pilgrimage site. It houses the highly revered 18th-century icon of Our Lady of Sorrows, Queen of Poland.

    An interesting fact about the basilica is its intentional numerical coding, where the architecture itself mirrors the calendar: 365 windows represent the days of the year, 52 doors symbolize the weeks in a year, and 12 columns represent the months of the year (as well as the Twelve Apostles).

  3. Basilica of the Uganda Martyrs, Namugongo

    This stunning church is built to look like a traditional African hut. It features 22 steel pillars to represent the 22 Catholic martyrs who lost their lives in 1886 for practicing the Christian faith.

  4. St. Peter's Cathedral Basilica (Ghana)

    The basilica features a distinct Victorian architectural style blended seamlessly with local Ghanaian cultural aesthetics. The spacious interior accommodates thousands of worshippers and is decorated with vibrant, modern stained-glass windows showcasing African motifs, biblical stories, and African saints. Its prominent altar, crucifix, and intricate sculptures reflect a rich cultural heritage.

    The basilica contains vibrant stained-glass windows that depict biblical scenes featuring characters with African features and skin tones instead of traditional European depictions.

Curated Favourites

An ordered collection of personal favourite captured exposures!

  1. Basilica of the Sagrada Familia

    🏛️ The Great Architectural Facts: The Tallest Church on Earth: Following the historic placement of a monumental cross atop the central Tower of Jesus Christ, the spire reached its maximum final height of 172.5 meters (566 feet). This officially makes the Sagrada Família the tallest church structure in the world, surpassing Germany's Ulm Minster.

  2. St. Peter's Basilica (Vatican City)

    St. Peter's Basilica, located in Vatican City, is one of the largest and most sacred Catholic churches in the world. Built on the traditional burial site of Saint Peter, it took 120 years to build and features the tallest dome in the world. Additionally, The Tomb of St. Peter: Deep inside the underground chambers (called the Vatican Grottoes) lies the traditional tomb of Saint Peter, the first Pope.

  3. Basilica of St Mary

    The Basilica of Saint Mary in Minneapolis, Minnesota is a landmark of immense religious and historical significance. Centrally located along Hennepin Avenue, this majestic structure stands as America's very first basilica. Pope Pius XI officially conferred this rare honor upon the parish in 1926, making 2026 its grand centennial anniversary year.

  4. Holy Family Minor Basilica

    The Cathedral Basilica of the Holy Family in Nairobi is a premier spiritual landmark and the seat of the Catholic Archdiocese of Nairobi. Located in the heart of the Central Business District (CBD) near City Hall Way and Parliament Road, it serves as the headquarters of the Catholic Church in Kenya. Nairobi’s First Stone Building: The original church structure, built on the site in 1904 by the Holy Ghost Missionaries to serve railway workers, was the very first stone building in Nairobi.
