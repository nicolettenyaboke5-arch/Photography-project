The History of the Basilica

A Preservation of the grandeur and heritage of Catholic Basilicas through images!

Fun Fact: In Ancient Roman architecture, a basilica (Greek basilike) was a large public building with multiple functions that was typically built alongside the town's forum. The basilica was in the Latin West equivalent to a stoa in the Greek East. The building gave its name to the basilica architectural form. |Home
